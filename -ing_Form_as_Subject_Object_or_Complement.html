-ing Form as Subject, Object or Complement

We can use the -ing form as the subject, object or complement of a clause, for example: - Smoking costs a lot of money - I don’t like writing. - My favorite occupation is reading. Sometimes the -ing form can also have an object itself. In this case, the whole expression [-ing + object] can be the subject, object or complement of a clause or sentence. - [Smoking cigarettes] cost a lot of money - I don’t like [writing letters] and I [reading emails]. - My favorite occupation is [reading detectives stories]
