use -ing

when the word is the subject of a clause:

  • Swimming is good exercise.
  • Doctors say that smoking is bad for you.

after a preposition:

  • I look forward to meeting you.
  • They left without saying goodbye.

after certain verbs:

  • avoid, dislike, enjoy, finish, give up, mind/not mind, practise

Look at these example sentences:

  • I dislike getting up early.
  • Would you mind opening the window?

Some verbs can be followed by the

infinitive or

  • ing

form without a big change in meaning:

begin, continue, hate, intend, like, love, prefer, propose, start

  • It started to rain / It started raining
  • I like to play tennis / I like playing tennis
